Rodent Control & Exclusion in Fort Lauderdale

Rats and mice are highly adaptable pests that easily find their way into South Florida homes and businesses. Our team provides complete trapping, sanitation, and permanent exclusion services to keep rodents out of your structure.

Common Signs of Rodent Control

  • Scratching or scampering noises in walls, attics, or ceilings, especially at night.
  • Dark, capsule-shaped droppings along baseboards, in cabinets, or near food sources.
  • Gnaw marks on wood, drywall, wires, and food packaging.
  • Smudge marks or grease streaks along walls and pipes.
  • Nesting materials like shredded paper, insulation, and fabric hidden in dark corners.

Why It Matters in South Florida

South Florida is particularly prone to roof rats, Norway rats, and house mice. The warm weather allows them to breed rapidly year-round. Rodents pose significant health risks by carrying pathogens like Salmonella and Leptospirosis, and their constant need to gnaw can damage electrical wiring, creating severe fire hazards in attics and crawlspaces.

Our Treatment Approach

We use a systematic three-step approach to resolve rodent issues. First, we conduct a thorough inspection to locate nesting areas and entry points. Second, we deploy strategic, safe trapping and removal measures. Finally, we sanitize affected areas to remove pheromones and health hazards, ensuring a safe environment.

Ongoing Prevention & Protection

The only permanent solution to rodents is exclusion. We seal all potential entry points larger than a quarter-inch using durable materials like steel mesh, concrete sealant, and metal flashing. We also advise on reducing outdoor attractants like overhanging tree limbs and open trash bins.

Residential & Commercial Capabilities

Rodent infestations can ruin a business's reputation and lead to health code violations, especially in restaurants and hotels. We offer discreet commercial rodent monitoring and exclusion, alongside our residential pest protection plans, to keep your space rodent-free.

Expert Knowledge

Rodent Control FAQs

Common questions about local extermination and prevention strategies for Rodent Control.

What is rodent exclusion?

Exclusion is the process of sealing up all physical entry points (holes, vents, gaps around pipes) that rodents use to enter a building. It is the most effective and permanent way to prevent future rodent problems.

How do roof rats get into homes?

Roof rats are excellent climbers and typically enter homes from above. They use overhanging tree branches, power lines, and roof vents to access attics and soffits.

Are your rodent treatments safe for pets?

Yes, we place all traps and bait stations in secure, tamper-resistant boxes that keep pets and children safe from contact.
